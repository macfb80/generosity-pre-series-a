Intelligent WTR Bottle — PP02 Smart Lid
The bottle that knows
if your water is
actually hydrating you.

The PP02 Smart Lid houses three sensors — TDS, temperature, and Time-of-Flight volume — and streams every intake event via BLE to the WTR App in real time. It's not tracking how much you drink. It's measuring whether what you drink is bioavailable enough to count.

📡
TDS Sensor — Water Quality Score
Measures total dissolved solids per intake event. Flags if source water is high-contamination before it's absorbed.
🌡️
Temperature Sensor — Absorption Signal
Cold water slows gastric absorption. The NHS algorithm adjusts bioavailability scoring based on drink temperature.
📏
ToF Volume Sensor — Verified Intake
Time-of-Flight measures exact mL consumed per sip. No self-reporting. No estimates. Verified intake that feeds NHS.
📶
BLE Smart Cap — Live App Sync
PP02 firmware v5. Bluetooth Low Energy streams TDS + mL + temp to WTR App within seconds of each intake event.

WTR App — The Intelligence Layer
The app that converts
telemetry into your
NHS™ Score.

The WTR App is the intelligence engine sitting on top of the hardware. It ingests BLE telemetry from the PP02 Smart Lid, cross-references water quality against EPA SDWIS data, syncs with 7 wearable platforms, and outputs a single actionable number: your Net Hydration Score. It knows if your HRV dropped because you're dehydrated — not just stressed.

NHS™ Algorithm — USPTO Provisional 4489-046P
NHS = Σ(Intake × Absorption) − Σ(Fluid Loss) + EAI
Intake × Absorption — mL consumed × bioavailability coefficient (TDS-adjusted, temp-adjusted)
Fluid Loss — Sweat + respiration, estimated via wearable skin temp + strain score
EAI — Electrolyte Adjustment Index, mineral balance offset from water quality profile
Output — Net liters: positive = optimally hydrated. Negative = dehydration deficit, with intervention prompt.
